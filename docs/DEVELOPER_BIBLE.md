# Nexus Earth Developer Bible

## Purpose

This is the master reference for future AI workers. It protects approved lore while allowing modular expansion.

## World Rules

Nexus Earth is set in 2095 after the Unraveling, Consolidation, Nexus Accord, and Polished Age. The world is divided between Sovereign Cities and Fractured Zones. Solenne is an approved Sovereign City, and The Fringe is an approved Solenne district. Do not create new cities unless explicitly approved.

## Lore Rules

Approved lore is source of truth. Robert Hit Dev is The First Unregistered and remains missing. The Gray Line operates between official and unofficial systems. Solenne Integrated Systems governs Solenne. Expansions may add supporting details, businesses, items, properties, settlements, and minor NPCs without changing these facts.

## Quest Rules

Do not rewrite approved quest chains. New quests must use stable IDs, explicit dependencies, rewards, failure states, and story impact notes. Quest content must respect player freedom and remember reputation outcomes.

## NPC Rules

Ezra Voss, Maren Solis, Sable Orin, and Cael are protected story anchors. Supporting NPCs may connect to them, but must not replace their roles or reveal core mysteries without an approved quest.

## Expansion Rules

Additive changes are preferred. Future cities, factions, and expansions require canon approval, region codes, database migration plans, and compatibility notes for Godot, FastAPI, PostgreSQL/Supabase, and Next.js.

## Content Creation Standards

Markdown must be structured, production-ready, and easy to convert into database rows. Every entry should include an ID, category, relationships, gameplay purpose, and lore notes where relevant.

## Naming Conventions

Use clear names that fit Solenne, The Fringe, the Fractured Zones, and Unregistered culture. Avoid joke names, duplicate names, and names that imply unapproved cities, factions, or main characters.

## Canon Rules

Canon has levels: approved core lore, approved quest chains, supporting content, implementation notes, and draft ideas. Lower levels cannot override higher levels. When uncertain, document the uncertainty and keep the change reversible.
